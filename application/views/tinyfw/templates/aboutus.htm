Our Story

Philosophy

Maiyet is a modern, ethical and luxury fashion brand that partners with global artisans to incorporate exquisitely handcrafted details and unexpected fabric techniques into our collections - all sourced globally and made locally. Headquartered in the heart of downtown New York, Maiyet partners with artisans in places such as India, Indonesia, Italy, Kenya, Mongolia, Peru, Bolivia, and Thailand. We rely heavily on the support of NEST, an independent not-for-profit organization dedicated to training and developing artisan businesses. 


Sustainable Luxury

Maiyet is deeply committed to forging partnerships with artisans that promote self-sufficiency and entrepreneurship in developing economies. To this effect, Maiyet creates open source supply chains. In order to ensure programmatic success for Maiyet and its partner companies, Maiyet has entered into strategic partnerships. NEST, an independent not-for-profit organization is dedicated to training and developing artisan businesses, works with the silk weaving communities in Varanasi, India. After partnering with the Gobi Revival Fund, Maiyet worked with the nomad goat herders of Outer Mongolia to create FAIR, the world's first ethically sourced and environmentally sustainable cashmere yarn in the world. Maiyet has been awarded The Butterfly Mark, powered by Positive Luxury, for our commitment to protect our planet's resources whilst taking pride in our craftsmanship, service and design.

Artisan Techniques

The team of Maiyet is motivated and empowered by the promise of preserving ancient artisan techniques and elevating the next generation of master craftsmen from places such as India, Indonesia, Italy, Kenya, Mongolia, Peru, Bolivia, Japan and Thailand. From textile weaving in Varanasi, India to sourcing cashmere fiber from Outer Mongolia, Maiyet has worked with the world's most extraordinary talents in techniques as diverse hand knitting, macrame, crochet, hand block print development, reverse appliqué, embroidery, batik, shibori, metal working and hand carving.

Hand Embroidery‎

Developed in India during the Mughal era, Aari is an intricate and labor-intensive embroidery technique, in which fabric is stretched taut over an adda or frame. Artisans use av tiny, awl-like hook to pull loops of thread through the fabric’s surface. Aari craftspeople have an immense repertoire of stitches, which Maiyet incorporates into designs to add texture and dimensionality to its garments.

​Reverse Appliqué​

Reverse Appliqué follows a tradition dating back generations across various regions of India, incorporating these time-honed skills into a modern design aesthetic. Crisp cotton is cut away to reveal a motif that has been handstitched onto the underside of the garment. 

Block Printing

To create Maiyet’s signature block printed textile patterns, a master craftsperson hand-chisels wood blocks into detailed designs. Each block is then carefully inked and placed onto the fabric in a precise, interlocking pattern. The artisan must apply uniform pressure to ensures a successful printing. The resulting textile is meticulously crafted and carries with it a palpable touch.

Hand Knitting

Dating back almost 10,000 years, has the longest continuous history of textile production in the world. Maiyet sources and partners with local Peruvian and Bolivian collectives, providing artisans with luxury Italian milled wool and cashmere blend yarns, which are subsequently hand-crafted into seasonal knitwear collections.

Handloom Silk Weaving

To create a jacquard textile, the desired pattern is coded onto a punch card that determines the placement of “warp” threads, strung vertically onto a loom. The loom operator conveys the horizontal “weft” thread through the warp on a “shuttle.” With each subsequent stroke, the delicate jacquard pattern emerges from the loom’s threads. Maiyet collaborates with master weavers in Varanasi who preserve the tradition of the hand-operated Jacquard loom to create finely wrought, original silks.

Metal Casting

Dating back over 3000 years, metal casting is a complex art that has played a critical role in the development and advancement of human cultures and civilization. The fundamental process involves pouring liquid metal into a mold and then allowing it to cool and solidify, but the entire technique may incorporate expertise in ceramics, sculpture and the material sciences. To create its line of hand-cast jewelry, Maiyet partners with master craftspeople in India, Kenya, Indonesia, Italy and France who have been plying their art for generations.

Horn and Bone Carving

Horn and bone are versatile and durable materials that have been employed in African and Indonesian jewelry craft for generations. To create Maiyet’s line of ethically sourced jewelry, the materials are carefully hand-sculpted into sinuous shapes and polished to a soft glow. These signature pieces embody a modern peripatetic lifestyle, effortlessly joining indigenous traditions with a luxuriously spare aesthetic.

Bag Leathercraft

Artisanal heritage meets modern design in Maiyet’s line of luxury leather goods. Sourced from the best Italian tanneries, hides are carefully inspected and selected for their durability, suppleness, and rich color. Predominantly made by hand using traditional techniques, each Maiyet piece is cut, pieced, sewn and finished in family-run Italian mills.
